Health insurance is a policy that covers medical expenses such as hospitalization, surgery, and treatment costs.

A premium is the amount paid periodically to maintain an insurance policy.

Coverage refers to the total amount the insurance company will pay for claims.

A deductible is the amount the policyholder must pay before the insurance starts covering expenses.

A sum insured is the maximum coverage amount available under an insurance policy.

Cashless hospitalization allows patients to receive treatment without paying upfront at network hospitals.

A network hospital is a hospital that has a tie-up with an insurance provider for cashless services.

A waiting period is the time during which certain conditions are not covered after purchasing a policy.

Pre-existing diseases are medical conditions that existed before purchasing the insurance policy.

Most health insurance policies cover pre-existing diseases after a waiting period of 2 to 4 years.

A claim is a request made to the insurance company to cover medical expenses.

Claim settlement ratio indicates the percentage of claims settled by an insurance company.

A higher claim settlement ratio generally indicates better reliability of the insurer.

A no-claim bonus is a reward given for not making any claims during a policy year.

Family floater insurance covers multiple family members under a single sum insured.

Individual health insurance covers only one person under the policy.

Family floater plans are cost-effective for families, while individual plans offer dedicated coverage per person.

Term insurance provides life coverage for a specific period and pays benefits only on death.

Life insurance provides financial support to beneficiaries in case of the policyholder's death.

Motor insurance covers damages to vehicles due to accidents, theft, or natural disasters.

Third-party insurance covers damages caused to other people or property.

Comprehensive motor insurance covers both third-party and own vehicle damages.

Travel insurance covers risks associated with traveling such as medical emergencies, trip cancellations, and lost baggage.

Critical illness insurance provides a lump sum payout for specified serious illnesses like cancer or heart attack.

Health insurance usually covers hospitalization, medicines, doctor consultations, and diagnostic tests.

Outpatient department (OPD) coverage includes medical expenses that do not require hospitalization.

Most policies do not cover cosmetic procedures unless medically necessary.

Insurance policies may exclude certain treatments or conditions known as exclusions.

Common exclusions include cosmetic surgery, self-inflicted injuries, and experimental treatments.

A grace period is the extra time allowed after the premium due date to make payment.

Policy renewal ensures continued coverage without losing benefits like waiting period credits.

Portability allows switching insurance providers without losing accumulated benefits.

A rider is an additional benefit that can be added to a base insurance policy.

Accidental death benefit rider provides extra payout in case of accidental death.

Hospital daily cash provides a fixed daily allowance during hospitalization.

Claim rejection can occur due to non-disclosure of information or policy exclusions.

Non-disclosure of medical history can lead to claim rejection.

Insurance fraud includes providing false information to obtain benefits.

A policy document contains all terms, conditions, inclusions, and exclusions.

Insurance helps reduce financial burden during medical emergencies.

Choosing insurance depends on age, health condition, and financial capability.

Higher premium plans usually provide higher coverage and additional benefits.

Young individuals typically benefit from lower premiums and early policy purchase.

Older individuals may face higher premiums due to increased health risks.

Family size influences the choice between individual and family floater plans.

Network hospital availability is important for smooth claim processing.

Policies with wider hospital networks offer better accessibility.

Health insurance does not usually cover pre-existing diseases immediately.

Claim process involves notifying insurer, submitting documents, and approval.

Cashless claims are processed directly between hospital and insurer.

Reimbursement claims require the user to pay first and get reimbursed later.

Medical inflation increases healthcare costs over time, making insurance essential.

Insurance acts as a financial safety net during unforeseen events.

Choosing the right policy requires comparing coverage, premium, and benefits.

Short waiting periods and high claim settlement ratios are desirable features.

Policies with low exclusions and flexible terms are generally better.
